Netlify Custom Domain Setup with Free Subdomain

Connect your is-pro.dev subdomain to a Netlify site, configure DNS records for both root and www variants, and set up automatic HTTPS provisioning through Netlify Lets Encrypt.

## Introduction As a developer on the is-pro.dev platform, you can take advantage of Netlify's free hosting features to deploy your static site. One of the key benefits of using Netlify is the ability to set up a custom domain, which allows you to access your site using a personalized URL instead of the default is-pro.dev subdomain. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of setting up a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify, using myproject.is-pro.dev as an example. ## Prerequisites Before you start, make sure you have the following: - A Netlify account - A site deployed on Netlify - A subdomain on is-pro.dev (e.g., myproject.is-pro.dev) - Access to your DNS settings (either through your domain registrar or a third-party DNS service) ## Setting Up Your Custom Domain To set up your custom domain, follow these steps: 1. Log in to your Netlify account and navigate to the Sites page. 2. Click on the site you want to set up the custom domain for (e.g., myproject). 3. Click on the Settings icon (represented by three horizontal lines) and select Domain management from the drop-down menu. 4. Click on Add custom domain and enter your custom domain (e.g., myproject.com). 5. Netlify will automatically generate a DNS record for your custom domain. You'll need to add this record to your DNS settings. 6. The DNS record will be in the format of `CNAME myproject.com your-netlify-subdomain.netlify.app` (e.g., `CNAME myproject.com myproject.is-pro.dev.netlify.app`). 7. If you're using a third-party DNS service, you can add the record manually. If you're using your domain registrar's DNS service, you may need to contact their support team for assistance. ## Configuring HTTPS Once you've set up your custom domain, you'll need to configure HTTPS to ensure that your site is secure. Netlify provides free SSL certificates for custom domains. To enable HTTPS, follow these steps: 1. In the Domain management section, click on the three dots next to your custom domain and select Edit. 2. Scroll down to the HTTPS section and click on the Verify DNS configuration button. 3. If your DNS configuration is correct, Netlify will automatically generate an SSL certificate for your custom domain. 4. Once the SSL certificate is generated, you can test your site by accessing it using the HTTPS protocol (e.g., https://myproject.com). ## Common Pitfalls When setting up a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify, there are a few common pitfalls to watch out for: - **DNS propagation delays**: It can take up to 48 hours for DNS changes to propagate globally. If you're experiencing issues with your custom domain, try waiting for a few hours and then checking again. - **Incorrect DNS records**: Make sure you've added the correct DNS record to your DNS settings. If you're unsure, you can contact Netlify support for assistance. - **SSL certificate issues**: If you're experiencing issues with your SSL certificate, try verifying your DNS configuration again or contacting Netlify support. ## Best Practices To ensure a smooth custom domain setup process, follow these best practices: - **Use a consistent naming convention**: Use a consistent naming convention for your custom domain and subdomain (e.g., myproject.com and myproject.is-pro.dev). - **Test your site regularly**: Test your site regularly to ensure that it's working correctly and that there are no issues with your custom domain or HTTPS configuration. - **Keep your DNS settings up to date**: Make sure your DNS settings are up to date and that you're using the correct DNS records for your custom domain. ## Conclusion Setting up a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify is a straightforward process that can be completed in a few steps. By following the instructions outlined in this guide, you can take advantage of Netlify's free hosting features and access your site using a personalized URL. Remember to test your site regularly and keep your DNS settings up to date to ensure a smooth and secure user experience. ## FAQ Q: Can I use a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify without setting up HTTPS? A: While it's technically possible to use a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify without setting up HTTPS, it's not recommended. HTTPS is an essential security feature that protects your site and its users from potential threats. Q: How long does it take for DNS changes to propagate globally? A: DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate globally. If you're experiencing issues with your custom domain, try waiting for a few hours and then checking again. Q: Can I use a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify if I'm using a third-party DNS service? A: Yes, you can use a custom domain with a free subdomain on Netlify even if you're using a third-party DNS service. You'll need to add the correct DNS record to your DNS settings, which can usually be done manually. Q: What happens if I encounter issues with my custom domain or HTTPS configuration? A: If you encounter issues with your custom domain or HTTPS configuration, you can contact Netlify support for assistance. They'll be able to help you troubleshoot the issue and provide guidance on how to resolve it.
