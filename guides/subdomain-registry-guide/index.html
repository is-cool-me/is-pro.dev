Everything you need to know about registering free subdomains on is-pro.dev — from choosing the right subdomain name to configuring DNS records and managing multiple projects.

Getting Started with is-pro.dev

is-pro.dev provides free subdomains for developers, creators, and small projects. This guide covers the full workflow — registration, DNS setup, and multi-domain management.

Step 1: Sign In with GitHub

Visit dash.is-pro.dev and click "Login with GitHub". Authorize the application to access your public GitHub profile — no private repository access is requested. Once authenticated, you are taken to your dashboard, which shows your current domains, credit balance, and available slots.

Step 2: Choose a Subdomain Name

Use the domain registration form to pick a name. Choose something descriptive like portfolio, api, or blog. The name must be between 3 and 20 characters and can contain letters, numbers, and hyphens. Check availability — the search performs a real-time DNS lookup.

Step 3: Select Your Zone and Records

Choose your zone: is-pro.dev or is-into.tech. Add the DNS records needed for your project — at minimum a CNAME or A record. You can add multiple record types (MX for email, TXT for verification, SRV for game servers). Each subdomain costs 3 credits to create.

Step 4: Manage Multiple Subdomains

The dashboard lists all your registered subdomains with their status, expiry date, and DNS records. You can edit records, renew domains (2 credits), or delete domains. Slot usage is tracked — the free tier includes 3 slots. Purchase additional slots for 5 credits each if needed.

Credits and Renewals

New accounts receive 3 free credits. Earn more by completing offers on the Rewards page (1 credit per completion). Domains expire after 30 days with a 7-day grace period. Renew before the grace period ends to avoid permanent deletion.

Best Practices

  • Use descriptive subdomain names for better SEO — search engines rank subdomains individually
  • Monitor expiry dates in the dashboard to avoid losing domains
  • Set up Cloudflare proxy (orange cloud) for free SSL on HTTP services
  • Keep DNS records minimal — remove unused records to reduce attack surface

FAQ

What happens when my subdomain expires?

DNS records are removed after 30 days. A 7-day grace period follows where you can renew for 2 credits. After the grace period, the subdomain is released for public registration.

Can I transfer my subdomain to another is-pro.dev user?

Subdomain ownership is tied to the GitHub account that registered it. There is no transfer mechanism — delete the domain and let the other user register it if available.
