Explainer

What exactly is a subdomain?

A subdomain is a label placed before a root domain, separated by a dot. In myproject.is-pro.dev, the word myproject is the subdomain. You choose that part; we own and manage the root domain.

Subdomains work just like full domain names for most purposes — you can point them at a web server, a CDN, an API, or an email provider using DNS records. They're widely used to separate services (e.g., api.example.com vs docs.example.com) or to give individuals a unique address under a shared domain.

With is-pro.dev, you get the flexibility of a real domain address without the cost of registering and maintaining your own top-level domain. Just pick a name, point it at your host, and go.

Supported DNS record types

Type Purpose Common use
A Maps to an IPv4 address VPS, dedicated server
AAAA Maps to an IPv6 address IPv6-enabled servers
CNAME Alias to another hostname Vercel, Netlify, GitHub Pages
TXT Arbitrary text data Verification, SPF records
MX Mail server routing Custom email for your subdomain

Record type availability is confirmed in the dashboard at registration time.

Naming rules

Your subdomain label must follow standard DNS naming conventions:

  • Only lowercase letters (a–z), digits (0–9), and hyphens (-) are allowed.
  • Cannot start or end with a hyphen.
  • Maximum 63 characters per label.
  • Must be unique within the chosen zone.
