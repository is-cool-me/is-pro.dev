Developer Portfolio Domain: Build Your Brand

Your developer portfolio is your digital business card. It is the first thing potential employers, clients, and collaborators see when they search for you online. Hosting it on a free is-pro.dev subdomain gives you a professional URL without the cost of a custom domain. This guide covers everything from choosing the right subdomain to optimizing your portfolio for search engines.

Why Your Portfolio Needs a Custom Domain

A portfolio hosted on username.github.io or a generic platform URL does not stand out. A subdomain like portfolio.is-pro.dev signals that you are serious about your craft. It is short, memorable, and professional. When you share it on resumes, LinkedIn, or business cards, it leaves a lasting impression.

Choosing Your Subdomain

Your subdomain should be simple and professional. Best practices include:

  • Using your name: john-doe.is-pro.dev
  • Using your brand: codercraft.is-pro.dev
  • Using your specialty: frontend-dev.is-pro.dev

    • Avoid numbers, hyphens in confusing places, or overly long names. Register your chosen subdomain at dash.is-pro.dev.

    Building Your Portfolio

    Start with a template if you want to launch quickly. Popular options include:

  • Astro: Excellent performance with built-in Markdown support. Use the portfolio template.
  • Next.js: Great for more complex portfolios with blog sections. Use the portfolio-starter template.
  • Hugo or Jekyll: Perfect for static sites that need minimal maintenance.
  • Plain HTML/CSS/JS: If you want full control without framework overhead.

    • Essential sections every portfolio should include:

  • Hero section with your name, title, and a brief tagline
  • About me describing your skills, experience, and what you are passionate about
  • Projects showcasing 3-6 of your best works with descriptions, technologies used, and live links
  • Skills listing your technical expertise (languages, frameworks, tools)
  • Experience with your work history or notable contributions
  • Contact with a form or direct links to email and social profiles
  • Blog (optional but highly recommended for SEO)

    • Deployment Options

    Cloudflare Pages integrates seamlessly with is-pro.dev since both use Cloudflare's infrastructure. Connect your GitHub repository, and Cloudflare Pages will deploy your site automatically on every push. SSL is automatic, and you get unlimited bandwidth.

    Vercel offers zero-configuration deployment and is ideal for Next.js portfolios. It provides automatic preview deployments for every pull request.

    GitHub Pages is free and simple. Push your static files to a gh-pages branch or use GitHub Actions for automated deployment.

    SEO for Your Portfolio

    Even a personal portfolio benefits from SEO optimization:

  • Use descriptive page titles and meta descriptions
  • Add alt text to all images
  • Create an SEO-friendly URL structure (use your subdomain)
  • Add structured data (JSON-LD) for your personal profile
  • Include a sitemap.xml
  • Ensure your site loads quickly (optimize images, minimize JS)
  • Publish blog posts about your projects to attract organic traffic

    • Performance Best Practices

    A fast portfolio keeps visitors engaged:

  • Optimize images with WebP format and appropriate sizes
  • Use lazy loading for images below the fold
  • Minimize CSS and JavaScript
  • Use system fonts or subset custom fonts
  • Enable HTTP/2 and Brotli compression (automatic with Cloudflare)

    • Adding a Blog

    A blog section dramatically improves your portfolio's SEO and demonstrates your expertise. Write about:

  • Technical tutorials related to your projects
  • Lessons learned from building specific features
  • Comparisons of tools and frameworks you have used
  • Industry insights and your perspective on trends

Each blog post should be at least 800 words, include code examples where relevant, and have a clear title and meta description.

Troubleshooting

My portfolio looks different on mobile: Test responsive design using browser DevTools. Ensure your CSS uses relative units and media queries.

My images are slowing down the site: Use an image CDN or convert to WebP format. Target under 100KB per image.

My contact form is not working: Use Netlify Forms, Formspree, or a serverless function to handle form submissions.

FAQ

Q: Should I host my portfolio on a subdomain vs my own domain?

A: A subdomain is perfect for getting started. If you later purchase a custom domain, you can redirect your subdomain to it.

Q: How many projects should I showcase?

A: Quality over quantity. Showcase 3-6 of your best, most diverse projects. Each should have a clear description and technologies used.

Q: Should I include client work or only personal projects?

A: Both, with permission. Client work demonstrates real-world experience. Personal projects show passion and initiative.

Conclusion

Your developer portfolio is one of the most important tools in your career. Hosting it on a free is-pro.dev subdomain gives you a professional, memorable URL. Focus on clean design, fast performance, and quality content. Update it regularly and share it proudly. Your next opportunity is just a click away.
