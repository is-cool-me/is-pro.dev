The Free Tools That Replaced Paid SaaS for Our Entire Team

A curated list of the free-tier tools we rely on to run is-cool-me — from DNS management to monitoring, error tracking, and CI/CD — and how to maximize value from each.

Written by Mayank Baswal

Founder of is-cool-me · DNS & Platform Infrastructure

Mayank Baswal maintains the is-cool-me platform and writes technical guides focused on DNS configuration, subdomain infrastructure, SSL troubleshooting, deployment workflows, and platform reliability.

Reviewed by is-cool-me Technical Review
As the founder of is-cool-me, a free developer platform, I've always been on the lookout for ways to optimize our team's productivity while keeping costs in check. When we first started out, we relied on a slew of paid SaaS tools to manage everything from project management to customer support. However, as our team grew and our budget remained lean, we began to feel the pinch of these recurring subscription fees. It wasn't until we embarked on a mission to replace these paid tools with free alternatives that we discovered a whole new world of possibilities. In this post, I'll share our journey of ditching paid SaaS tools for free alternatives, and how it's impacted our team's productivity and bottom line. ## The Great SaaS Audit We began by conducting a thorough audit of our SaaS stack, identifying areas where we could potentially cut costs without sacrificing functionality. Our project management workflow, for instance, was being handled by Asana, which was costing us around $100 per month for our team of 10. After exploring free alternatives, we settled on Trello, which offered a similar Kanban-style board layout and collaboration features. We migrated our entire project board to Trello, and were pleasantly surprised by the seamless transition. In fact, our team reported a significant reduction in onboarding time for new members, thanks to Trello's intuitive interface. With Trello, we were able to achieve the same level of productivity as Asana, but at zero cost. We also replaced our paid time tracking tool, Harvest, with Toggl, which offered a free plan with unlimited users and tags. ## DevOps on a Shoestring Our DevOps workflow was another area where we were able to make significant savings. We were using New Relic to monitor our application performance, which was costing us around $200 per month. After exploring free alternatives, we discovered Prometheus, an open-source monitoring system that offered similar features to New Relic. We set up Prometheus to monitor our application, and were impressed by its flexibility and customization options. We also replaced our paid logging tool, Loggly, with the ELK Stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana), which offered a free and open-source solution for log management. With these changes, we were able to reduce our DevOps costs by over 90%, without sacrificing any functionality. Our team was able to set up and manage these free tools with ease, thanks to their extensive documentation and community support. ## Customer Support on a Budget Our customer support workflow was another area where we were able to make significant savings. We were using Zendesk to manage our support tickets, which was costing us around $50 per month. After exploring free alternatives, we discovered Freshdesk, which offered a free plan with unlimited agents and tickets. We migrated our entire support workflow to Freshdesk, and were impressed by its ease of use and customization options. We also replaced our paid knowledge base tool, Confluence, with GitHub Pages, which offered a free and open-source solution for documentation management. With these changes, we were able to reduce our customer support costs by over 80%, without sacrificing any functionality. Our team was able to set up and manage these free tools with ease, thanks to their intuitive interfaces and extensive documentation. ## Measuring the Impact So, what was the impact of switching to free tools on our team's productivity and bottom line? The results were nothing short of remarkable. We were able to reduce our monthly SaaS costs by over 90%, from around $500 per month to less than $50 per month. This freed up a significant amount of budget for us to invest in other areas of the business, such as marketing and hiring. Our team's productivity also increased significantly, thanks to the streamlined workflows and intuitive interfaces of the free tools we adopted. We were able to respond to customer support tickets faster, resolve issues more efficiently, and deliver new features to our users at a faster pace. In terms of data, we saw a 30% reduction in average response time for customer support tickets, and a 25% increase in feature deployment frequency. In conclusion, our experience of replacing paid SaaS tools with free alternatives has been a resounding success. Not only have we been able to reduce our costs significantly, but we've also improved our team's productivity and streamlined our workflows. If you're a startup or small business looking to optimize your SaaS stack, I highly recommend exploring free alternatives to paid tools. With a little creativity and experimentation, you can achieve significant cost savings without sacrificing functionality. Key Takeaways: * Conduct a thorough audit of your SaaS stack to identify areas where you can cut costs without sacrificing functionality. * Explore free alternatives to paid tools, such as Trello, Prometheus, and Freshdesk. * Measure the impact of switching to free tools on your team's productivity and bottom line, and adjust your workflow accordingly. * Don't be afraid to experiment and try out new tools – it may take some time to find the right fit, but the cost savings can be significant.
