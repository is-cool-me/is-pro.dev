The Free Tools That Replaced Paid SaaS for Our Entire Team

A curated list of the free-tier tools we rely on to run is-pro.dev — from DNS management to monitoring, error tracking, and CI/CD — and how to maximize value from each.

As the founder of is-cool-me, a free developer platform, I've always been on the lookout for ways to optimize our team's productivity while keeping costs in check. Like many startups, we initially relied on a mix of paid SaaS tools to manage our workflow, collaborate on projects, and monitor our infrastructure. However, as our team grew and our needs evolved, we began to feel the pinch of rising subscription fees. It wasn't until we embarked on a mission to replace our paid SaaS tools with free alternatives that we discovered a whole new world of possibilities. ## Embracing the Free and Open-Source Ecosystem Our journey began with a thorough audit of our existing toolset. We identified areas where we could potentially replace paid SaaS with free tools, such as project management, customer support, and monitoring. One of the first tools we replaced was Trello, which we used for project management, with Taiga, a free and open-source alternative. Taiga offered many of the same features as Trello, including Kanban boards, Gantt charts, and issue tracking, but at no cost. We were able to migrate our existing projects to Taiga with minimal disruption, and our team quickly adapted to the new interface. Another significant replacement was swapping our paid Zendesk account with Zammad, a free and open-source customer support platform. Zammad offered a similar feature set to Zendesk, including ticketing, chat, and knowledge base management, but with the added benefit of being customizable to our specific needs. ## Streamlining DevOps with Free Tools As a developer platform, our DevOps workflow is critical to our operations. We used to rely on paid tools like New Relic and Datadog for monitoring and logging, but we found that we could achieve similar results with free tools like Prometheus and Grafana. Prometheus, a popular monitoring system, allowed us to collect metrics from our infrastructure and applications, while Grafana provided a powerful visualization platform for our data. We also replaced our paid Loggly account with the ELK Stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, and Kibana), which offered a more scalable and customizable logging solution. By leveraging these free tools, we were able to gain better insights into our infrastructure and application performance, all while reducing our costs. For example, we used Prometheus to monitor our API latency and identify bottlenecks, which allowed us to optimize our code and improve our overall user experience. ## Measuring Productivity Gains and Cost Savings The switch to free tools has had a significant impact on our team's productivity and our bottom line. By replacing our paid SaaS tools with free alternatives, we've been able to reduce our monthly subscription fees by over 70%. This has allowed us to allocate more resources to critical areas of our business, such as development and marketing. In terms of productivity, our team has reported a significant reduction in time spent on tool management and maintenance. With free tools like Taiga and Zammad, we've been able to streamline our workflow and focus on higher-value tasks. We've also seen an improvement in our overall collaboration and communication, thanks to the customizable nature of our new toolset. For instance, we've been able to integrate our Taiga project management platform with our Zammad customer support platform, allowing us to track customer issues and project tasks in a single interface. ## Overcoming Challenges and Lessons Learned While the transition to free tools has been largely successful, we've encountered some challenges along the way. One of the biggest hurdles was finding suitable replacements for our paid SaaS tools. It took some time and effort to research and evaluate different options, but ultimately, we were able to find free tools that met our needs. We also had to invest some time in configuring and customizing our new toolset, but this has paid off in the long run. Another lesson we learned was the importance of evaluating the total cost of ownership when considering free tools. While the upfront cost may be zero, there may be other costs associated with implementation, maintenance, and support. However, in our experience, the benefits of free tools have far outweighed the costs. For example, we've been able to customize our ELK Stack logging solution to meet our specific needs, which has allowed us to gain better insights into our application performance and identify areas for improvement. In conclusion, our experience with replacing paid SaaS tools with free alternatives has been overwhelmingly positive. By embracing the free and open-source ecosystem, streamlining our DevOps workflow, and measuring productivity gains and cost savings, we've been able to optimize our team's workflow and reduce our costs. While there have been some challenges along the way, the benefits of free tools have far outweighed the costs. If you're considering making the switch to free tools, I encourage you to take the leap and explore the many options available. Key Takeaways: * Free tools like Taiga and Zammad can replace paid SaaS tools for project management and customer support * DevOps workflow can be streamlined with free tools like Prometheus and Grafana * Evaluating the total cost of ownership is crucial when considering free tools * Customization and configurability are key benefits of free and open-source tools Related Resources: * Taiga: https://www.taiga.io/ * Zammad: https://zammad.org/ * Prometheus: https://prometheus.io/ * Grafana: https://grafana.com/ * ELK Stack: https://www.elastic.co/what-is/elk-stack * Free and Open-Source Software Directory: https://directory.fsf.org/
