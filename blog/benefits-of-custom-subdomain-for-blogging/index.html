The Benefits of Using a Custom Subdomain for Your Blog or Website

Explore the advantages of using a custom subdomain for your blog or website, including improved branding and organization. Learn how a custom subdomain can enhance your online presence and simplify content management.

Written by Mayank Baswal

Founder of is-cool-me · DNS & Platform Infrastructure

Reviewed by is-cool-me Technical Review
As the founder of is-cool-me, a free developer platform, I've seen countless bloggers and website owners struggle with establishing a strong online presence. One often-overlooked aspect of building a professional website is the use of a custom subdomain. In my experience, a custom subdomain can be a game-changer for bloggers and website owners, offering a range of benefits that can enhance their online presence, improve organization, and even boost their brand. However, I've also seen many developers and bloggers neglect to utilize custom subdomains, often due to a lack of understanding about their advantages. In this article, I'll delve into the benefits of using a custom subdomain for your blog or website, sharing concrete examples and data to illustrate the value of this often-underutilized technique. ## Introduction to Custom Subdomains A custom subdomain is a subdomain that is specifically created for a particular purpose or section of a website. For example, instead of using a generic URL like `www.example.com/blog`, a custom subdomain could be `blog.example.com`. This may seem like a small difference, but it can have a significant impact on the user experience and the overall professionalism of a website. At is-cool-me, we use a custom subdomain for our blog, `blog.is-cool-me.com`, which allows us to separate our blog content from the rest of the website and create a unique branding experience for our readers. By using a custom subdomain, we've been able to improve the organization of our content, making it easier for users to find the information they need. ## Benefits of Custom Subdomains for Blogging One of the primary benefits of using a custom subdomain for blogging is the ability to create a unique branding experience. A custom subdomain allows bloggers to establish a distinct identity for their blog, separate from the rest of their website. For example, the popular blogging platform Medium uses a custom subdomain for each of its publications, such as `onezero.medium.com` and `gen.medium.com`. This creates a sense of professionalism and authority, which can be particularly important for bloggers who are looking to establish themselves as experts in their field. Additionally, custom subdomains can improve the organization of blog content, making it easier for users to find specific articles or topics. At is-cool-me, we've seen a significant increase in user engagement since implementing our custom subdomain, with users spending more time on our blog and exploring more content. ## Custom Subdomain Advantages for Website Owners Custom subdomains can also offer a range of advantages for website owners, beyond just blogging. One of the key benefits is the ability to use subdomains for organization, creating separate sections of a website for different types of content or services. For example, the popular website builder Wix uses a custom subdomain for its user dashboard, `editor.wix.com`, which allows users to access their website's backend and make changes. This creates a clean and intuitive user experience, making it easier for users to manage their websites. Custom subdomains can also be used for branding purposes, creating a unique and memorable URL that reflects a company's identity. At is-cool-me, we've seen many of our users create custom subdomains for their websites, such as `store.example.com` or `community.example.com`, which helps to establish a strong brand presence online. ## Using Subdomains for Organization and Branding In addition to the benefits mentioned above, custom subdomains can also be used to improve the overall organization and branding of a website. By creating separate subdomains for different sections of a website, owners can create a clear and logical structure that makes it easy for users to find what they're looking for. For example, the popular e-commerce platform Shopify uses a custom subdomain for its user dashboard, `admin.shopify.com`, which allows merchants to manage their online stores. This creates a sense of professionalism and authority, which can be particularly important for businesses that are looking to establish themselves online. At is-cool-me, we've seen many of our users create custom subdomains for their websites, such as `blog.example.com` or `forum.example.com`, which helps to establish a strong brand presence and improve the overall user experience. In conclusion, using a custom subdomain for your blog or website can offer a range of benefits, from improving organization and branding to creating a unique and memorable URL. By leveraging custom subdomains, bloggers and website owners can establish a strong online presence, improve user engagement, and create a professional and authoritative brand identity. Whether you're a seasoned developer or just starting out, I highly recommend exploring the use of custom subdomains for your website or blog. Key Takeaways: * Custom subdomains can improve the organization and branding of a website, making it easier for users to find what they're looking for. * Using a custom subdomain can create a unique and memorable URL that reflects a company's identity and establishes a strong brand presence. * Custom subdomains can be used for a range of purposes, from blogging and e-commerce to community-building and user engagement. * By leveraging custom subdomains, website owners can establish a professional and authoritative brand identity, which can be particularly important for businesses and entrepreneurs. Deployment scenario from operations: At is-cool-me, we recently worked with a client who was looking to create a custom subdomain for their blog. The client was using a generic URL, `www.example.com/blog`, and wanted to create a more unique and memorable URL that reflected their brand identity. We worked with the client to create a custom subdomain, `blog.example.com`, which allowed them to establish a distinct identity for their blog and improve the overall user experience. The client saw a significant increase in user engagement and website traffic, and was able to establish a strong brand presence online. Common mistakes: * Not using a custom subdomain for blogging or website content * Not creating a clear and logical structure for subdomains * Not using subdomains for branding purposes * Not testing and verifying the functionality of custom subdomains How to verify it works: 1. Test the custom subdomain URL to ensure it is resolving correctly and directing users to the correct content. 2. Verify that the custom subdomain is properly configured and set up in your website's DNS settings. 3. Test the user experience and ensure that the custom subdomain is creating a unique and memorable URL that reflects your brand identity. 4. Monitor website analytics to track user engagement and website traffic, and adjust the custom subdomain as needed to optimize performance.
